# Creative Brief: Visual Hook Library for Real Estate Short-Form Video

## Project Overview

We're building a web app that adds hooks and branding to raw real estate property videos for short-form platforms (TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts). Your job is to create a **library of 12 pre-produced visual hook templates** using AI video generation tools that production operators can select and attach to the beginning of broker property videos.

These hooks are the scroll-stoppers — the first 3-6 seconds are strategically critical for capturing attention before a viewer swipes past.

## Context: Why Hooks Matter

- TikTok's own guidance: prioritize the hook in the first 6 seconds, introduce core proposition in the first 3 seconds ([source](https://ads.tiktok.com/help/article/creative-best-practices))
- Listings with drone reels generate 53% higher click-through rates
- "Hook stacking" (visual + text + audio layered together) is the single most powerful technique
- 85% of viewers watch without sound — every hook must work visually even on mute
- The goal is broker brand-building (getting more listings), not selling houses
- Longer videos (60-90s) can outperform ultra-short ones IF the opening is strong (Socialinsider 2025 data)

## Target Audience

Dutch real estate brokers who aspire to Serhant-level personal branding (reference: serhant.com). The brokers range from luxury boutique (Amsterdam-Zuid) to broad market (suburban family homes). The hooks must work across this spectrum.

**Cultural note:** Dutch audiences prefer directness over hype. Avoid "American influencer" energy. Aim for aspirational but grounded — elevated, not exaggerated.

## What You're Creating

12 visual hook templates across 3 tiers, organized by priority.

Each hook template is a **reusable motion graphic / video element** (1.5-3 seconds) that gets placed at the start of a property video. Some hooks include space for dynamic text overlays (price, location, hook line). The broker's branding may or may not appear in the hook — see branding notes per template.

## Tier 1: Core Hooks (Build First)

These 4 hooks are the foundation. They cover the most effective formats identified in research.

### Hook 1: "The Drone Reveal"
- **What:** Cinematic aerial shot that dramatically reveals a property or neighborhood, synced to a beat drop
- **Duration:** 2.5-3 seconds
- **Visual:** Starts tight or obscured (clouds, trees, rooftop edge), pulls back or rises to reveal the full property/street/canal
- **Text overlay zone:** Bottom third — space for neighborhood name or price ("Amsterdam-Zuid | EUR 875K")
- **Audio cue:** Designed to sync with a beat drop at the 1.5-second mark
- **Branding:** None in hook. Branding starts after the reveal transitions into the property footage
- **Variations needed:** 2-3 visual styles (urban canal, suburban green, coastal)
- **Reference:** SERHANT Studios opening shots, DSTRCT Real Estate aerial content
- **AI tools to consider:** Runway Gen-3, Kling, Luma Dream Machine for generating cinematic aerial footage

### Hook 2: "The Walk-In"
- **What:** First-person POV approaching and entering a property — front door opens to reveal interior
- **Duration:** 2-3 seconds
- **Visual:** Camera moves forward (walkway, garden path, hallway), door opens or camera crosses threshold. The transition from exterior to interior IS the hook moment
- **Text overlay zone:** Center or top — space for a one-line hook ("Dit huis heeft een geheim")
- **Audio cue:** Door sound effect or ambient-to-music transition at the moment of entry
- **Branding:** None. Pure immersion
- **Variations needed:** 3 styles (modern front door, classic Dutch grachtenpand entrance, villa driveway approach)
- **Reference:** POV property tour trend on TikTok, indoor drone flythrough aesthetic
- **AI tools to consider:** Runway Gen-3 for generated walk-throughs, or filmed template with AI enhancement

### Hook 3: "The Bold Frame"
- **What:** Full-screen typographic statement that commands attention, then cuts to property footage
- **Duration:** 1.5-2 seconds
- **Visual:** Bold, large text on a solid or subtly textured background. Text appears with impact (slam, typewriter, or kinetic animation). Background color adapts to broker's brand palette
- **Text overlay:** IS the hook — dynamic text field for hook lines like "Het beste huis onder 5 ton in Utrecht" or "Raad eens wat dit kost"
- **Audio cue:** Text impact synced to a percussive hit or bass note
- **Branding:** This is the one hook where branding CAN appear — small broker logo in corner. But it's optional, operator decides
- **Variations needed:** 3 animation styles (slam impact, smooth fade-in, kinetic type)
- **Reference:** Bold caption trends on TikTok, news-style text hooks
- **Production note:** This is primarily a motion graphics template, not AI video. After Effects / Remotion territory

### Hook 4: "The Detail Zoom"
- **What:** Extreme close-up of a luxury detail (kitchen tap, marble countertop, herringbone floor, garden view through window) that pulls back to reveal the full room
- **Duration:** 2-3 seconds
- **Visual:** Macro/close-up shot, shallow depth of field, slow pull-back or rack focus to reveal context. Highlights trending real estate features (the "copper tap" playbook concept)
- **Text overlay zone:** Bottom — space for feature callout ("Italiaans marmer | Bulthaup keuken")
- **Audio cue:** Subtle ambient sound (water running, light piano note) transitioning to main track
- **Branding:** None
- **Variations needed:** 3-4 detail types (kitchen feature, bathroom detail, architectural element, outdoor/garden)
- **Reference:** Product photography style applied to real estate, luxury brand advertising
- **AI tools to consider:** Runway, Kling for generating photorealistic detail shots with camera movement

## Tier 2: Variety Hooks (Build After Core Is Proven)

These 4 hooks add variety to the library once the core hooks are validated.

### Hook 5: "The Split Screen"
- **What:** Two-panel comparison that creates instant curiosity
- **Duration:** 2-3 seconds
- **Visual:** Left panel vs right panel. Could be: exterior vs interior, street view vs living room, this property vs average property, before vs after (staging/renovation)
- **Text overlay:** Center divider — "Buiten" / "Binnen" or "EUR 300K" / "EUR 800K"
- **Branding:** None
- **Variations needed:** 2 styles (vertical split, horizontal split)

### Hook 6: "The Neighborhood Flyover"
- **What:** Fast aerial sweep over a neighborhood/city, zeroing in on a specific location
- **Duration:** 2.5-3 seconds
- **Visual:** Wide drone shot of cityscape or neighborhood, camera swoops down and zooms toward a specific street/building. Google Earth zoom-in energy but cinematic
- **Text overlay zone:** Location pin or neighborhood name appearing as camera descends
- **Audio cue:** Whoosh sound effect synced with the zoom movement
- **Branding:** Small logo watermark in corner (optional)
- **Variations needed:** 2-3 city types (Amsterdam canals, modern suburban, coastal town)

### Hook 7: "The Lifestyle Flash"
- **What:** Rapid montage of lifestyle moments associated with the property — not the house, but the LIFE in the house
- **Duration:** 2-3 seconds (4-5 quick cuts, 0.5 seconds each)
- **Visual:** Morning coffee on terrace, kids in garden, dinner party in dining room, cycling to work from the neighborhood. Aspirational but grounded (Dutch, not Hamptons)
- **Text overlay:** Single line appearing after the montage: "Jouw volgende thuis"
- **Audio cue:** Quick cuts synced to beat, music drops in on the text reveal
- **Branding:** None
- **Reference:** SERHANT lifestyle framing, "sell the life, not the house"

### Hook 8: "The Data Card"
- **What:** Animated infographic/stat card that presents a surprising number
- **Duration:** 1.5-2 seconds
- **Visual:** Clean, modern data visualization — a number animates up (counter effect), a chart bar fills, or a percentage rings up. Minimal design, maximum readability
- **Text overlay:** IS the hook — "Huizenprijzen in [stad] +12% dit jaar" or "Slechts 3 dagen op de markt"
- **Audio cue:** Subtle tick/counter sound effect
- **Branding:** Background color adapts to broker palette (optional)
- **Production note:** Motion graphics template, not AI video

## Tier 3: Personality Hooks (Build Once Audience Is Established)

These 4 hooks lean into personality and storytelling. They're riskier but have highest viral potential.

### Hook 9: "The Confession"
- **What:** Agent-in-frame, direct to camera, vulnerable or surprising opening line
- **Duration:** 2-3 seconds
- **Visual:** Close-up of agent, slightly off-center framing, natural lighting. Feels intimate and unscripted
- **Text overlay:** Subtitle of the opening line
- **Audio cue:** No music — just voice. Music drops in after the hook
- **Note:** This hook requires the broker to film themselves. Provide a shot composition template and script framework, not a fully generated visual
- **Script examples:** "Ik had deze klant bijna het verkeerde huis laten kopen" / "Dit is het duurste huis dat ik ooit heb verkocht"

### Hook 10: "The Scroll-Stop Pattern Break"
- **What:** Something visually unexpected that breaks the feed rhythm — a visual non-sequitur that makes viewers pause
- **Duration:** 1-1.5 seconds
- **Visual:** Could be: a door slamming open, a drone flying through a window, a match-cut from something mundane to something extraordinary, a visual glitch/distortion that resolves into property footage
- **Audio cue:** Abrupt sound effect — slam, whoosh, record scratch
- **Branding:** None
- **Variations needed:** 3-4 different pattern break concepts
- **Reference:** TikTok pattern interrupt trend, transition compilations

### Hook 11: "The Question Card"
- **What:** Direct question to viewer, designed to generate comments
- **Duration:** 1.5-2 seconds
- **Visual:** Text-on-screen question with animated appearance. Background can be blurred property footage or solid color
- **Text overlay:** IS the hook — "Hoeveel denk je dat dit huis kost?" / "Zou jij hier willen wonen?"
- **Audio cue:** Notification-style ping or subtle tension sound
- **Branding:** None — engagement-first
- **Production note:** Motion graphics template

### Hook 12: "The Cinematic Mood"
- **What:** Pure atmosphere — a moody, cinematic moment that sets tone before the property tour begins
- **Duration:** 2-3 seconds
- **Visual:** Golden hour light through windows, rain on glass, morning mist in a garden, candlelight in a living room. Slow motion, color graded, dreamlike
- **Audio cue:** Ambient sound (rain, birds, silence) with music fading in
- **Branding:** None — the aesthetic IS the brand
- **Variations needed:** 3-4 moods (warm morning, dramatic evening, rainy atmospheric, bright and airy)
- **Reference:** Architectural Digest mood pieces, cinematic real estate photography

## Technical Requirements

### Format & Specs

| Element | Spec |
|---------|------|
| Aspect ratio | 9:16 (vertical) — 1080x1920px minimum |
| Duration | 1.5-3 seconds per hook (see individual specs above) |
| Frame rate | 30fps (standard) or 60fps (for slow motion elements) |
| File format | ProRes 422 or high-bitrate MP4 (H.265) for source; MP4 for delivery |
| Color space | Rec. 709, with room for color grading to match broker brand tones |
| Text safe zones | Keep text away from top 15% (platform status bars) and bottom 20% (platform UI, captions) |

### Dynamic Elements (where applicable)

Some hooks have zones for dynamic content that the app will populate:
- **Text fields:** Hook line, price, location, feature callout (marked in specs above)
- **Color fields:** Background or accent colors that adapt to broker brand palette
- **Logo zone:** Corner placement, optional (marked in specs above)

Deliver these as separate layers or with clear masking so the app can swap content programmatically.

### Platform-Specific Notes

| Platform | Hook consideration |
|----------|-------------------|
| TikTok | Hook in first 3-6 seconds (official TikTok guidance). Core proposition in first 3 seconds. Trending audio sync is critical. Casual energy > polish. Native-feeling execution rewarded. |
| Instagram Reels | Reels up to 3 minutes eligible for non-follower discovery (Explore, Reels tab). Text hook should appear early. Competing in mixed feed. Polished aesthetic preferred. |
| YouTube Shorts | Capture attention in "first few seconds" (official YouTube guidance). Shorts up to 3 minutes supported. Optimize for re-watchability — algorithm weights re-watches. |

### Audio Guidelines

- Each hook should include a suggested audio bed or sound effect layer
- Design for audio-OFF viewing (text overlays must carry the hook alone)
- Include sync markers for beat-drop alignment with trending tracks
- Voiceover hooks (script hooks) are handled separately via ElevenLabs — your visual hooks should leave space for voiceover layering where applicable

## Anti-Patterns (What NOT to Do)

Based on research into what kills hooks:

- **No logo intros.** Never open with a broker logo bumper. The hook's job is to stop the scroll, not identify the brand
- **No slow pans.** The first frame must have movement or visual tension. Static = scroll
- **No generic openers.** "Check dit uit!" or "Kijk tot het einde!" are instant skips
- **No information overload.** The hook is an invitation, not the entire message. One idea per hook
- **No American hype energy.** Keep it aspirational but grounded. Dutch audiences reject performative enthusiasm
- **No clickbait without payoff.** If the hook promises something, the subsequent property footage must deliver

## Deliverables

### Per hook template (x12):

1. **Final rendered hook** — Production-quality video file (ProRes + MP4)
2. **Source project file** — After Effects / Runway / tool-specific project with editable layers
3. **Layer map** — Document showing which layers are dynamic (text, color, logo zones)
4. **Audio file** — Separated audio bed / sound effect (WAV)
5. **Usage guide** — 2-3 sentences on when to use this hook (which property type, which platform, which broker style)

### Overall:

6. **Hook selection guide** — Matrix showing which hooks work best per platform and property type
7. **Style consistency document** — Ensuring all 12 hooks feel like they belong to the same system despite variety

## Production Approach

These hooks should be created using **AI video generation tools** (Runway Gen-3, Kling, Luma Dream Machine, Pika, etc.) combined with **motion graphics** (After Effects, Remotion) for text overlays and dynamic elements.

The expectation is not hand-filmed footage — it's AI-generated cinematic content enhanced with professional motion design. The quality bar is: "this looks like it was shot by a professional drone operator / cinematographer" even though it was generated.

For hooks that involve agent-in-frame (Hook 9: "The Confession"), provide shot composition templates and guidelines rather than generated faces.

## Research Highlights (Inform Your Creative Decisions)

Key findings from platform research (full document available on request):

- **Hook stacking works:** Layering visual + text + audio hooks simultaneously engages multiple cognitive systems — nearly impossible to ignore
- **Curiosity > everything:** Curiosity-gap hooks outperform all other types consistently across platforms
- **Drone content wins:** 53% higher CTR for drone reels vs static content
- **Dutch tone:** Direct, slightly understated, dry humor welcome. 20% more energy than conversation, but never performative
- **Content mix:** 50% curiosity-driven / 30% aspirational / 20% authority-educational
- **85% watch muted:** Text overlays aren't optional — they ARE the hook for most viewers
- **Cut every 2-3 seconds:** Visual change rhythm that resets attention
