# Bright River — Language & Tone

Bright River writes in direct, results-oriented B2B copy. Confident, specific (numbers everywhere), benefit-led rather than feature-led.

---

## Signature Vocabulary

### Cost
- "Lowest cost" / "lowest total cost"
- "Keep your margins high"
- "Cost-effective solutions"
- "Without breaking the bank"
- "Lower cost per listing"
- "At a fraction of the cost"

### Speed
- "Fast, consistent" (appears on both home hero and RE hero)
- "24H turnaround"
- "Incredibly fast"
- "Never miss a deadline"
- "Reduce your time to web"
- "Quality results within 24 hours"

### Quality
- "High quality" / "high-quality"
- "Consistent quality"
- "On-spec, on-brand"
- "First time right"
- "Ready to publish"
- "Highest e-commerce quality standards"

### Operations
- "Work smarter, deliver faster"
- "Effortless workflows"
- "Stress-free solutions"
- "Seamless integration"
- "No disruption"
- "Fully optimized standard workflows for speed and quality at scale"
- "Standardized"

### AI
- "AI-powered" (used very frequently — almost a brand prefix)
- "AI-driven"
- "AI-Human hybrid"
- "AI-powered workflow"
- "AI-powered precision"

### Scale
- "High volume"
- "High complexity"

### Trust / Risk Removal

- "No-cost revisions"
- "SLA-guaranteed"

## Recurring Section Headers

- "Let's talk numbers" — their go-to metrics section header
- "Trusted by 1000+ brands and retailers worldwide" — social proof bar
- "How can we help?" — closing CTA section
- "Where would you like to start?" — solution navigation
- "Here's what sets us apart" — differentiator section
- "How it works" — process explanation

---

## Tone Observations

- **Metric-heavy** — they quantify nearly every claim
- **Business outcomes** — speaks to margins, revenue, SLAs, KPIs
- **Low jargon** — avoids technical terms except where it signals expertise (REST API, FTP)
- **Warm touches** — occasional ("absolute breeze", "we'd love to hear from you") but mostly professional
- **Action-oriented CTAs** — always low-commitment: "Test for free", "Schedule a 20-minute call", "Get started for free"
- **Never hype** — no "revolutionary", "game-changing", "disruptive" — they are pragmatists

## Guidelines for ReelForge Demo Copy

When presenting to Bright River:

1. **Mirror their outcome language** — "deliver faster", "lower cost per listing", "no re-shoots", "consistent quality"
2. **Speak to operational scale** — volumes (listings/day, turnaround hours, rework rates), not one-off projects
3. **Quantify everything** — they are metrics-first; vague claims will not land
4. **Lead with real estate** — their RE vertical is strategic; position ReelForge as purpose-built for that workflow
5. **Emphasise the AI-Human hybrid** — they already trust this framing; don't oversell full automation
6. **Remove friction in the demo flow** — their CTAs are always low-commitment; the demo itself should feel "risk-free"
7. **Use their vocabulary**: "workflow", "SLA", "turnaround", "listing", "seamless", "standardized", "cost per listing"
8. **Avoid** marketing-speak they don't use: "revolutionary", "game-changing", "disruptive"

**Key personas to address:** Head of Production (operational efficiency), Head of Photography (fewer re-shoots, faster editing), CEO/founder (margins and revenue growth).
