## Industry-Specific Insights

### Fashion (Largest Segment)
- POSED + Editing dominate — on-model imagery is the primary conversion driver
- Ghost mannequin / invisible mannequin is a high-volume standard workflow
- Color variants via recoloring eliminate the need to photograph every SKU color
- Case study: Lucardi (jewelry retailer) — posed on-model imagery for all categories

### Furniture
- 3D & AR are the primary value drivers (visualize in your space before buying)
- Virtual photography replaces expensive lifestyle photo shoots
- Rendered Shorts for social media marketing of furniture collections
- Lifestyle renders for contextual product presentation

### Jewelry
- Virtual models + retouching are critical (macro detail, on-body context)
- Etika Jewels case study: combined POSED and 3D for on-model jewelry imagery
- High complexity editing (reflections, transparency, tiny details)

### Real Estate (Growth Vertical)
- Speed and cost per listing are the primary value drivers
- Dedicated solution page, named team, standalone positioning
- Target buyers: photography companies/agencies, not individual agents
- Differentiated by day-to-dusk and rain removal capabilities
- "Shadow production period" for risk-free onboarding

### FMCG / Consumer Goods
- Mobile Ready Hero Images (MRHI) — the primary product for CPG/FMCG
- NielsenIQ/Brandbank partnership (visual content standardization)
- Unilever as referenced test case (24% uplift with MRHI)
